Spatial Autocorrelation in California

This document assess the Global and Local Moran's I for distance travelled in California from December 2019 to November 2020. Three plots are shown below: the LISA Map, Stacked LISA Map and Average Distance Per County.

LISA Map

The LISA Map shows significant hot or cold spots in the given period of time. Note that many weeks do not have significant hot or cold spots.

Stacked LISA Map

The Stacked LISA Map is a cumulative version of the LISA Map with more recent hot spots or cold spots being shaded in with a darker color.

Average Distance Per County

The Average Distance Per County map shows the counties' standard deviation from the weekly mean. Switching between the LISA Map and the Average Distance Per County map on weeks with hot or cold spots demonstrates the meaning of hot and cold spots. By doing this, you can clearly see that a hot or cold spot has a high or low value along with its neighboring counties.

Global Moran's I

Global Moran's I is a measure of spatial autocorrelation, with values greater than zero indicating spatial clustering and values below zero indicating spatial dispersion. Since for each week the Global Moran's I is significant and positive, this means that there is spatial clustering between the high and low values for each week.

Local Moran's I

Our Global Moran's I estimate indicates that there is clustering of high and low values, so to find the hot and cold spots, we use the Local Moran's I. The p-value for this test is calculated by randomly permutating the neighborhood values to see if the counties' Local Moran's I test was just chance or not.

False Discovery Rate

The False Discovery Rate has been proposed as a more conservative way to identify significant values than just using the randomized Local Moran's I test as described above[1]. The False Discovery Rate is used in the LISA maps in order to reduce false positives and find the centers of hot or cold spots.

Data and Processing

The data used for these plots come from the bucketed_distance_traveled column of the Safegraph Social Distancing Metrics dataset. A weighted average of this column is used to account for methadological differences between versions of the data set. The rest of the steps are shown in this Jupyter Notebook.
